# Adventures of Sinbad! — advantage play notes

_Two-seat **Azure Gaming** cabinet with dual visible bonus meters and a fantasy map chase (Skeleton → Snake → Cyclops) into a jackpot **opportunity**. Sync via `npm run slots:sync`._

## Source narrative

Trip-report framing (January 2017): [Advantage play continued — Adventures of Sinbad](https://travelupdate.com/advantage-play-continued-adventures-sinbad/) on TravelUpdate. Cross-check producer materials from Azure Gaming where available; meter & jackpot ladders change by jurisdiction.

## Visible state worth scouting

At the shared top marquee you can typically read **monster progress** toward a jackpot-qualified path. Rational scouting focuses on **how much map + RNG stands between you** and:

1. A fight with the remaining boss (often **Cyclops** positioning matters most).
2. A **successful** boss kill (not automatic).
3. A **successful** jackpot draw if the wedge game triggers.

Dominant scouting cue from field writeups: **two of three bosses already defeated**, and Cyclops sits **few spaces away** — not stuck three spaces early with an overshoot risk.

## Mechanics (conceptual — verify ROM)

Collect **three “Adventure Time”** icons to spin a wheel (author cites **1–6** moves). Moves advance a party along a themed map landing on monsters. Clearing all three earns a jackpot **chance** spin (not a deterministic award).

Trip report cites **overshoot risk** (jump past Cyclops waiting for another trip around), battle RNG misses, and small consolation pays on jackpot wheels (example ladder on **50¢** included **$5 / $10 / $30 / $50 / $100 / Jackpot**).

## Returns (anecdotal)

Monster clears described as modest credit wins (**~5×–8×**) while jackpot wedges scale hard (**≈10× up past 1000×** anecdotes). Variance dominates — treat anecdotes as hypotheses.

## Locations

Author places it alongside other **tall novelty** pods (easy glass read) but more often **off-strip / mid-tier** banks than marquee Strip competition fields.
